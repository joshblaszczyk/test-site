A family trade

Three generations
of tailors.

My grandfather was a tailor. My father was a tailor. I'm a tailor. The trade got handed down the way trades used to — standing at the bench next to him, learning the feel of the leather and the bite of the needle.

The materials changed; the principles didn't. Pattern it to the actual piece. Cut once. Stitch where it counts. Build it the way it's supposed to be built.

A quote that found me

"I fix other people's mistakes."

A lot of what comes through the door is a re-do. A cushion that came out of the chain shop already pulling at the seams. A convertible top installed crooked. A headliner hanging in three months. Glue where there should have been stitches; particle board where there should have been marine ply.

It gets stripped, patterned again, and built right. By the time it leaves, you wouldn't know there was a first time.
